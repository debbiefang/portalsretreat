You may be wondering

Questions, answered.

Many of the women who join us have never tried the medicine before, and for some, this was their first time traveling too. We go through a safety assessment to make sure you're okay to work with the medicine, and as soon as you book, we send you a preparation guide with everything you need for your journey. You'll be guided by expert facilitators who create a safe, supportive space for everyone, whether it's your first time with plant medicine or your fiftieth.

The safety assessment is how we make sure you're fit to do the work with the medicine. We review any medication contraindications together. Some conditions aren't recommended, including antidepressants (such as SSRIs or MAOIs) or antipsychotic medications, psychiatric conditions or family histories of certain mental health disorders, surgery within the past three months, heart conditions, uncontrolled high blood pressure, uncontrolled diabetes, epilepsy, active addiction, or pregnancy. If any of these apply to you, participation may still be possible, so the best thing to do is message us and we'll talk it through.

Feeling nervous is normal, that's what we're here for. When you book, you'll receive a preparation checklist and a guide on how to work with the medicine, so you have peace of mind knowing you're supported. You'll also have access to us for any questions along the way. Got more questions? Email Debbie at debbieverse@gmail.com.

We hold a group integration call once we've all returned to our lives. We also have a WhatsApp group for the women who join each year, so everyone can stay in touch. Many have gone on to meet up with each other in different cities again.

Our circles are intentionally small, so you have personalized care and deeper bonds with one another. You are never pressured into anything you don't feel like participating in. We have exercises and agreements in place so everyone feels supported and seen.
